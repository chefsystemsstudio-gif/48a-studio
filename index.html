Questions

Before you ask.

Common questions about the manuals, who they are for, and how they work.

Who are these manuals written for?+

Private kitchen professionals at all stages — chefs transitioning from commercial kitchens, experienced freelance chefs, and permanent household chefs managing sole-charge positions. The manuals assume professional kitchen competency and focus entirely on the private household context that culinary training never covers.

What format do I receive?+

A PDF delivered immediately upon purchase. No account required, no platform to log into. You download it once and keep it. Works on any device.

Is this relevant outside of a specific country?+

Yes. The manuals address the professional dynamics, operational structures, and career realities of private household service — consistent across international markets. The intelligence is transferable.

Will there be more manuals in the series?+

Yes. Series 01 covers five manuals total. No.2, No.3, and No.5 are in production. Join the list to be notified at launch.

Who writes 48A?+

Anna Rens is a pen name used by a working private household professional with over a decade of sole-charge experience at UHNW level. The editorial perspective is entirely from inside the household — not from a recruiter, consultant, or agency.

05 · Confidentiality & Liability
Confidentiality (Standard Clause) The Chef agrees to maintain strict confidentiality regarding the identity, personal details, lifestyle, dietary requirements, household staff, property details, and any other personal or operational information relating to the Client and their household. This obligation continues indefinitely after the engagement ends. The Chef will not disclose, publish, or reference any aspect of the engagement on social media, in professional profiles, or in any public forum without prior written consent from the Client.
Liability Disclaimer (Standard Clause) The Chef carries personal professional liability and operates as an independent contractor, not an employee. The Client is responsible for ensuring the kitchen environment, equipment, and facilities meet applicable health and safety standards. The Chef shall not be held liable for allergic reactions where dietary requirements were not disclosed in writing prior to service. The Chef's total liability under this agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid for the engagement.
06 · Agency Arrangements (if applicable)
Direct Engagement Clause Where this engagement is sourced through a staffing agency, the Client agrees not to directly re-engage the Chef for a period of 12 months following the conclusion of this engagement without the knowledge and written consent of the agency, or without the Chef's agreement to a direct engagement fee equivalent to the agency's standard placement fee.
